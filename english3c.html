Branch B:


Frederik Hendrik Constant van Motman


Maybe Frederik Hendrik Constant van Motman111, head of branch B can best be characterized as the most colourful man with the most extensive offspring. In his time he was known as a bon vivant and a Casanova. During his marriage with many children he procreated on two other women another three children. One of his hobbies was the hunt on big game. He was member of  the Nimrodclub, an exclusive hunting party. It is very probably to owe to his way of live that the slogan “Van Motman without champagne is the Netherlands without Oranje” came into being. This slogan grew later popular and was first heard in 1861 during a walk of mr H.D. Levyssohn Norman with mr N.P. van den Berg and other people from the estate Tjampea to the solfataras112 of the Salak.      

He was knight in the Order of the Netherlands Lion and lord and owner of the estates Semplak, Tjikandi Oedik and Kedong Badak. In 1846 he was member of the freemasons’ lodge “Ster in het Oosten’ in Batavia. He rendered many services to the government, among others he quelled the insurrection on the estate Tjikandi.

He leased from the heirs of Willem Vincent Helvetius van Riemsdijk the estate Tjampea c.a., viz Tjampea, Tjiboenboelan and Sadeng East. From 1882 he paid a rent of Fl 202.000,-- p.a. This rent was brought down over the years 1886 and 1887 till fl 150.000 due to financial difficulties113 and ended on 01-01-1888. According to a family comment his grave was canonized by the people. During the occupation by the japs the grave has been looted and got lost.  


It is remarkable that Pondok Gedeh has been recorded as his birthplace while this land didn’t belong to his father but to Nicolaas Engelhard, councillor of the East Indies. Probably Gerrit and Nicolaas got along good, under that circumstances a stay with each other is quickly arranged.


When he was 24 he married the 18 year old Sophia Maria van Riemsdijk, a adoptive daughter of Pieter W.H. and Sophia Cornelia Bangeman, a sister of his mother. The married couple got nine children. However FHC had also children with other women. Before his marriage he had a son and a daughter from a relation with the five years older TAN Nieo and 20 years later – during his marriage – another son from the same relation. This son could not be recognized because he was born out of wedlock; nevertheless he always carried the name Van Motman. Furthermore he had a son and a daughter out of a relation with TAN Kang Nio, also born out of wedlock. These children grew up at the homes of brothers of FHC.


Childeren


To make things clear I placed the children in chronological sequence, at first the six daughters:

  • Frederika Constantina, * Djamboe 1831 from Tan Nieo, married Pieter J.F. ten Cate and had seven children.

  • Sophia Maria, * Buitenzorg 1834, married Pieter J. Mess and had 12 children.

  • Jacoba Maria, * Buitenzorg 1839, married Folkert J. ten Cate, a brother of Pieter J.F., and had no children.

  • Carolina Catharina, * Dramaga 1842, married her nephew G.W.C. of  branch A and had 11 children.

  • Cornelia Charlotte, * Nanggoeng 1844 from Tan Kang Nio, adopted by Willem R. of branch A. She married Erland Th. van Bronckhorst and had no children.

  • Henriette Dorothea, * Dramaga 1848, married Albertus J. van Bronckhorst, a brother of Erland, and had eight children.

Two of the six girls had no children but the other four had no fewer than 38 children. Furthermore it is striking that two girls married a Ten Cate and two a Van Bronckhorst.

Then the eight sons in chronological sequence:

  • Willem Reinier jr, * Djamboe 1833 from Tan Nieo, had a relation with Rowanda and only one son who died early.

  • Jacob Gerrit Theodoor, * Dramaga 1844, married Ida and had five children, of whom two sons with male offspring; placed in branch B1.

  • Hendrik Pieter, * Nanggoeng 1846 from Tan Kang Nio, married Theodora E. Schiff and had six children, placed in branch E while he was raised by Pieter Cornelis.

  • Reinier Jacob, * Dramaga 1846, married Johanna A. Leidelmeyer and had 13 children of whom five sons with male offspring, placed in branch B2.

  • Cornelis Frederik Johan, * Dramaga 1851, married his cousin Geertruida W. of branch D, had three children, of whom one son with male offspring, placed in branch B3.

  • Charles Constant Theodoor, * Batavia 1853, married Josephine M. Leidelmeyer, a sister of Johanna A., had eight children, of whom one son with male offspring, placed in branch B4.

  • Frederik Hendrik, * Buitenzorg 1851 from Tan Nieo, had six children of whom two sons with male offspring, placed in branch B5.

  • Frederik Herman Johan, * Batavia 1856, died before April 2nd ,1888 without having had children.

It is remarkable that five children were born at Dramaga in the period 1842 till 1851, so at the home of grandmother. And that two brothers married with sisters Leidelmeyer.   

Frederik Hendrik Constant van Motman
