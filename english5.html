Summary of the Book



In De Familie Van Motman Constant has described in the first place the genealogy of its large  family, in itself already no small performance. The book contains however more than the genealogic data of the family. It has been interlarded with context tales, anecdotes and more than 100 illustrations of which approx. 40 in colour.   

The Van Motmans have entered the history as a well-known family of planters in West Java. So their lives form the principal part of the book.  Special is that this large East Indian family descends from a 17 year old boy, Gerrit Willem Casimir, who decided in 1790 to make his fortune in the former Netherlands East Indies.


The book starts with the descent of the family. Unlike the genealogists who investigated earlier the origin of the family, the author sees no connection with the three brothers Motman who had been raised to the peerage in 1627. Presumably the ancestors came initially from the Southern Netherlands and emigrated after the fall of Antwerp (1585) to the north. The first ancestor of whom something is known was called Bartelt (born around 1615/1620). After Bartelt came Michiel and after him Barthel. Gerrit W.C. was a grandson of Barthel. The family settled down in Mook and vicinity.


Gerrits family was Protestant, not without means and loyal to the House of Oranje. This last applied in particular to his uncle and tutor Gerrit Willem.  Gerrit W.C. was not the only member of the family who went to the former Netherlands Indies. In 1807, his older brother Frits was sent as a military man to Batavia, where he was killed in 1812. His grandson, known as Frisian Frits, inventor of the rinollinne, was the last representant of the family branch, who stayed in the Netherlands. Gerrit W.C. married in 1809 with Reiniera Bangeman. They got 13 children of whom only five sons remained in life. Those five became the heads of the branches A, B, C, D and E. Branch B is by far the largest and has been split in the branches B1 till B5.
