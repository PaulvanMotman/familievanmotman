Branch A:


Willem Reinier van Motman



Applying strictly the then legislation branch A would have been called illegitimate because Willem Reinier, head of this branch was born in the time that his parents were both still  involved in a divorce procedure and therefore not yet legally separated. Willem Reinier was born (September 4th,1802) seven years before their marriage. According to the law no recognition and legalization could take place by the marriage of his parents on  February 5th, 1809.

But GWC did adopt him on  March 23nd,  1808. As mother was the free non-Christian woman Cecilia registered (102). The name of this woman was practically certain fictitious. WWvR mentioned this in NL1903, p. 286, and used the following arguments to show that Reiniera was really the actual mother:

1. the certificate of death of Willem mentions Reiniera as his mother;

2. GWC and Reiniera lived already separated of table and bed of their first wife/husband respectively in 1797 and 1795;

3. Willem Reinier had the Christian names of his father and his mother;

4. in the family he has always been known as a full brother of the other four.

To this row could be added that the descendants of Willem Reinier participated for the same part in the heritage of Reiniera as his four brothers.


About the live of Willem not much is known. Even a picture of him does not exist. At first he was lord of the country Nanggoeng (also known as Tjoeroeh Bitoeng) and later lord of the country  Roempin. Nanggoeng came at a given moment in the  hands of branch E, whereas Roempin remained always in branch A.

In 1833 he was member of a  freemasons’ lodge in Batavia. It is unknown if he was member of `La Vertueuse or of `La Fidèle Sincérité (103). Furthermore he adopted a little girl, who was born in 1846 at the home of its brother Frits out of wedlock. And like his brothers Jacob of branch C are and Piet of branch E he was one of the 215 signers of the address to the king on  May 25th,1848 to protest against the manner of appointment of East Indian civil servants. A half year later he died at the age of 46. His early dead is possible one of the reasons that we do not know much of him. Although his wife  survived him 40 years of her is not much known either.

When he was 23, Willem married the just 17-year old Charlotte Geertruida Arnold. She was a daughter of Johann Friedrich, who had arrived from Germany in 1803 as provisional assistant in VOC-service with the ship `Minerva’ ;  later he belonged to the notability of Batavia.

Her sister Anna Maria married in 1825 Adrianus Johannes (Arnold) Bik, whereas her youngest brother Jan Willem (1813-1885) married in 1837, Augustina Michiels. He was the founder of the so-called Michiels Arnoldlanden (Tjilingsie, Tjibaroesa, Tjipamienkies, Tanah-Baroe and Klappa Noenggal). Augustina was a adoptive daughter (from his relation with the Balinese woman Thalia) of Augustijn Michiels  (`Majoor Jantje’); the daughters out of this marriage ` the girls Arnold’ were  in their time famous beauties.

As appears from  a photograph, which is in the possession of the Royal Netherlands Institute of Southeast Asian and Caribbean Studies in Leiden, Willem and his wife lived in a large mansion in the Laan de Riemer in Tanahabang ( Weltevreden). The couple had two daughters, who married descendants from a Frisian  family, the brothers Wijnand and Rutger van Slooten, and only one son, Gerrit Willem Casimir Jr. This son married his niece Carolina Catharina van Motman, a daughter of  Frederik H.C., head of branch B. Out of  this marriage 11 children were born among which only one son, Carolus Gerardus who had also 11 children (out of three marriages), of which five sons.


102 According to Prof.mr W.F. Prins in NL 1958, k. 258/259 it was not difficult in that time to find a not-Christian woman, who was prepared to function for the occasion as mother .

103 Both lodges were united in 1837 under the name `De Ster in het Oosten’.
